Bob Marley

The man who brought reggae to the world

Here's a timeline of Bob Marley's life:

  • 1945 - Born Robert Nesta Marley in Nine Mile, Jamaica
  • 1957 - Moves to Kingston with his mother, experiences inner-city life hardships
  • 1962 - Records first singles, "Judge Not" and "One Cup of Coffee"
  • 1963 - Forms The Wailers with Peter Tosh and Bunny Wailer
  • 1966 - Marries Rita Anderson, moves briefly to the U.S.
  • 1972 - Signs with Island Records; album Catch a Fire released internationally
  • 1973 - Releases Burnin' with hits like "Get Up, Stand Up" and "I Shot the Sheriff"
  • 1974 - The Wailers disband; Marley continues with Bob Marley and the Wailers
  • 1975 - Releases No Woman, No Cry, gaining international fame
  • 1976 - Survives an assassination attempt amidst political unrest
  • 1977 - Diagnosed with melanoma; releases Exodus, later named Album of the Century
  • 1978 - Receives UN Peace Medal of the Third World
  • 1980 - Releases Uprising with final hits, performs last concert
  • 1981 - Passes away at 36, laid to rest in Jamaica as a national icon
